## Background

The onboarding flow was created to help new users set up their account faster and understand which features were most relevant to their needs. Previously, the setup experience needed to be more guided, intuitive, and self-service so users could complete their initial configuration with less dependency on internal support.

This project focused on improving the first-time user experience by making the onboarding process clearer, more structured, and easier to complete.

## Problem Statement

New users needed a clearer setup flow to understand what information they had to provide and why it mattered. Without a structured onboarding experience, users could miss important setup steps, feel less guided, and require more assistance before fully using the product.

The challenge was to design an onboarding flow that felt simple and guided, while still collecting enough information to personalize the user’s account and feature recommendations.

### Key Issue
- Users needed clearer guidance during initial setup.
- The setup process had to collect important information without feeling overwhelming.
- The flow needed to support personalization based on role, industry, and main problems.
- The experience had to feel self-service, not dependent on manual support.

## Goal & Scope

To design a clean and intuitive onboarding flow that welcomes new users, motivates them to complete their first setup, and helps personalize the product experience based on their needs

## Process & Solution

### Step 1 / Requirement Review
I started by reviewing the PRD provided by the product team to understand the onboarding goals, target users, and required information. From there, I mapped the user flow to define how users would move through each setup step.

### Step 2  / Wireframe & Structure
After understanding the requirements, I created wireframes for a step-by-step onboarding experience. The structure was designed to reduce cognitive load by breaking the setup process into smaller, focused questions.

### Step 3  / UI Design & Guided Interaction
I designed the onboarding interface using a clean single-question layout, a top progress indicator, and selectable role options to make the setup process feel guided and easy to complete. Each step focused on one decision at a time, helping users stay focused without feeling overwhelmed.
The progress bar at the top gave users a clear sense of where they were in the onboarding flow, while the disabled “Continue” button encouraged them to complete the required selection before moving forward. I also included a “Skip Onboarding & Go to Advanced Setup” option to give more flexibility for users who preferred to bypass the guided flow.

## Summary

After restructuring and re-documenting the components, the design system became easier to maintain, reference, and reuse. Designers could work with clearer component standards, while product owners had a more accessible reference when reviewing UI decisions.

Although the process is still ongoing, this improvement helped build a stronger foundation for UI consistency, design collaboration, and future product scalability
